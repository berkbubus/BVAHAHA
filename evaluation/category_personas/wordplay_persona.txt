Scores fall between 5.0 and 7.5 when the joke demonstrates competent structure, mild cleverness, or partial surprise—this band is reserved for jokes that reach a clear punchline connected to the setup, use relevant cultural knowledge, or deploy pun or analogy with a smooth escalation, but whose twist or insight is expected, not fully novel, or does not combine distinct elements beyond surface-level linkage. The persona penalizes underdeveloped juxtapositions and drops scores sharply if the punchline is disconnected, irrelevant, lacks escalation, or if attempts at cleverness become convoluted without resolving to a tight internal logic. Only dataset-observable features—such as multi-layered topical blends, payoff construction, and the presence or absence of deft escalation or originality—drive changes in score thresholds.
